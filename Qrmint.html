3. QR Code Types

QR Mint supports a wide range of QR code types to meet various needs. Each type is optimized for specific information and can be fully customized to match your design requirements.

3.1 URL QR Codes

URL QR codes direct scanners to a specific website or web page. These are the most common type of QR codes used for marketing, product information, and general web links.

Use Cases:
  • Marketing materials directing to landing pages
  • Product packaging linking to instructions or registration
  • Business cards linking to portfolios or websites
  • Print advertisements with calls to action
How to Create:
  1. Select the "URL" tab from the QR type options
  2. Enter the complete website URL including "http://" or "https://"
  3. Customize appearance as desired
  4. Preview and export
Pro Tip: For better tracking and analytics, consider using URL shorteners or campaign tracking parameters before generating your QR code.

Example Input

Website URL:

https://example.com/landing-page

This URL will open directly when the QR code is scanned

3.2 vCard QR Codes

vCard QR codes contain contact information that can be instantly saved to a phone's contact list when scanned. This eliminates the need for manual data entry and potential errors.

Use Cases:
  • Business cards and networking materials
  • Email signatures
  • Company directories
  • Conference badges
How to Create:
  1. Select the "vCard" tab from the QR type options
  2. Fill in the contact details fields (Name, Phone, Email, etc.)
  3. Add optional fields like Company, Title, Website
  4. Customize appearance as desired
  5. Preview and export
Pro Tip: Include only essential contact information to keep the QR code complexity lower, which improves scan reliability.

Example Input

Name: John Smith

Title: Marketing Director

Company: Acme Inc.

Phone: +1 (555) 123-4567

Email: john@example.com

Website: www.example.com

Address: 123 Business St, City

This information will be formatted as a vCard that can be instantly saved to contacts

3.3 WiFi QR Codes

WiFi QR codes allow users to connect to a wireless network instantly by scanning the code, eliminating the need to manually enter complex passwords.

Use Cases:
  • Guest WiFi access in offices
  • Hotel and hospitality WiFi information
  • Cafés and restaurants offering customer WiFi
  • Event venues with temporary networks
How to Create:
  1. Select the "WiFi" tab from the QR type options
  2. Enter the network name (SSID)
  3. Select the security type (WPA/WPA2, WEP, or None)
  4. Enter the password (if applicable)
  5. Toggle "Hidden Network" if your WiFi SSID is not broadcast
  6. Customize appearance as desired
  7. Preview and export
Security Note: WiFi QR codes contain your network password in an encrypted format. Only display these in secure locations accessible to intended users.

Example Input

Network Name (SSID): GuestWiFi

Security Type: WPA/WPA2

Password: Welcome2023!

Hidden Network: No

When scanned, this will automatically connect compatible devices to the WiFi network

8. Best Practices

Follow these guidelines to ensure your QR codes are effective, scannable, and provide a good user experience.

Design Best Practices

  • Maintain high contrast between foreground and background
  • Keep logos small (under 30% of QR code size)
  • Ensure adequate quiet zone (margin) around the code
  • Test scan after any customization
  • Use error correction level appropriate for customization level
  • Avoid distorting QR code proportions

Implementation Best Practices

  • Size QR codes appropriately for viewing distance
  • Add a clear call-to-action near the QR code
  • Place QR codes in accessible, well-lit locations
  • Avoid placing on curved surfaces when possible
  • Test with multiple devices and scanning apps
  • Consider the destination experience (mobile-optimized?)

QR Code Placement Guidelines

Medium Minimum Size Placement Considerations
Business Cards 1.2 × 1.2 cm Back side, lower corner
Flyers/Brochures 2 × 2 cm Near contact information or CTA
Product Packaging 2.5 × 2.5 cm Flat surface, avoid seams or folds
Posters/Signage 5-10 cm Eye level, with clear instructions
Window Displays 10+ cm Visible from typical viewing distance

QR Code Testing Checklist

  1. Scan with at least 3 different devices (iOS, Android, etc.)
  2. Test with both built-in camera apps and dedicated QR scanners
  3. Verify scanning works at different distances
  4. Check scanning under different lighting conditions
  5. Test printed QR codes after production
  6. Confirm destination content loads properly on mobile
  7. Verify any tracking or analytics are capturing data

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I ensure my QR code is scannable?

A: Maintain high contrast between foreground and background colors, ensure adequate quiet zone (margin) around the code, avoid excessive customization that might compromise scan reliability, and always test your QR code with multiple devices before implementation.

Q: What size should my QR code be?

A: The optimal size depends on scanning distance. As a rule of thumb, QR codes should be at least 2 × 2 cm for close scanning (e.g., business cards) and scaled up based on viewing distance. For posters or displays meant to be scanned from 1-2 meters away, aim for at least 10 × 10 cm.

Q: Can I change what my QR code links to after creation?

A: Yes, but only with dynamic QR codes, which are a Premium feature. Dynamic QR codes can be edited to point to different content without needing to replace the physical QR code. Static QR codes (included in the free version) cannot be changed after creation.

Q: How do I track QR code scans?

A: QR Mint Premium offers built-in scanning analytics. Alternatively, when using URL QR codes, you can create links with UTM parameters for tracking in your web analytics platform, or use URL shorteners that provide click analytics.

Q: Will customizing my QR code affect its scannability?

A: Yes, excessive customization can potentially reduce scan reliability. QR codes have built-in error correction, but significant modifications like large logos or low contrast colors can make codes difficult to scan. Always test thoroughly after customization.

Q: Can I print my QR code on dark backgrounds?

A: Yes, by inverting the QR code colors. Use a dark background with a light-colored QR code pattern. Ensure there's still sufficient contrast and maintain the quiet zone with appropriate coloring.

Q: What information can be stored in a QR code?

A: QR codes can store various types of information including URLs, text (up to about 3000 characters), contact information (vCard), WiFi credentials, email addresses, phone numbers, and more. The amount of data affects the complexity of the QR code pattern.

Q: Are QR codes secure?

A: Standard QR codes are not encrypted, so sensitive information should not be encoded directly. For secure applications, use QR codes that link to secure, authenticated websites rather than embedding sensitive data in the code itself.

10. Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues with QR Mint or with the QR codes you've created, use this troubleshooting guide to identify and resolve common problems.

QR Code Not Scanning

Problem Possible Causes Solutions
QR code doesn't scan at all
  • Poor contrast
  • Insufficient quiet zone
  • Excessive customization
  • Increase contrast between colors
  • Add larger margin around code
  • Reduce logo size or remove it
  • Generate a new code with less customization
QR code scans inconsistently
  • Border color issues
  • Low resolution output
  • Different scanning apps
  • Use higher contrast colors
  • Export at higher resolution
  • Test with multiple scanning apps
Printed QR code won't scan
  • Print quality issues
  • Size too small
  • Glossy finish reflection
  • Print at higher resolution
  • Increase QR code size
  • Use matte finish materials
  • Avoid curved surfaces

Technical Issues

Generator Issues

  • Problem: QR code preview not loading
    Solution: Try refreshing the page, clear browser cache, or use a different browser
  • Problem: Unable to customize colors
    Solution: Check for browser compatibility, ensure JavaScript is enabled
  • Problem: Download button not working
    Solution: Check download permissions, try a different browser, or use the share option instead

Account Issues

  • Problem: Unable to register account
    Solution: Check email format, ensure password meets requirements, verify email
  • Problem: Can't access Premium features
    Solution: Verify subscription status, check payment method, contact support
  • Problem: Saved QR codes not appearing
    Solution: Refresh the dashboard, check account login status, clear cache

Before Contacting Support

Try these general troubleshooting steps:

  1. Clear your browser cache and cookies
  2. Try using a different web browser
  3. Disable browser extensions that might interfere
  4. Check your internet connection
  5. Verify that JavaScript is enabled in your browser
  6. Try accessing QR Mint from a different device
Contact Support: If you're still experiencing issues after trying the troubleshooting steps above, contact QR Mint support at support@qrmint.com or through the Help section in your account dashboard.
